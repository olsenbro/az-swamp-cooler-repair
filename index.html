Swamp Cooler Repair in the East Valley — Explained

What is swamp cooler repair?

Swamp cooler repair (also called evaporative cooler repair) fixes whole-house or rooftop units that cool air by pulling it through water-soaked pads. In Arizona's dry climate, a working cooler can drop indoor temperature 15–25°F. Common repairs include pad replacement, pump and motor service, float valve fixes, and spring startup after winter storage.

How much does swamp cooler repair cost in Mesa?

In Mesa and the East Valley, typical repair costs run $75–$150 for pad replacement, $100–$200 for a pump, $75–$125 for a belt or float valve, and $200–$450 for a motor. Spring startup service is usually $75–$175. Actual quotes depend on unit size and parts needed.

Why is my swamp cooler blowing hot air?

The most common causes are a failed pump (pads stay dry), clogged distribution tubing from East Valley hard water, outside temps above ~105°F, low fan speed, or worn pads. In Gilbert and Chandler, mineral buildup is especially common and often fixed with a pump flush or pad replacement.

What's the difference between a swamp cooler and an evaporative cooler?

They are the same system. "Swamp cooler" is the Southwest nickname; "evaporative cooler" is the technical term. Both pull hot outside air through water-soaked pads to drop temperature 15–25°F — ideal for Arizona's dry climate from March through June.
