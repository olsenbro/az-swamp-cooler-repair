Common Swamp Cooler Questions

Quick answers for East Valley homeowners. See all FAQs →

What's the difference between an evaporative cooler and a swamp cooler?

Nothing — they're the same thing. "Swamp cooler" is the common Southwest nickname; "evaporative cooler" is the technical term. Both pull hot air through water-soaked pads to drop temperature by 15–25°F in Arizona's dry climate.

Why is my swamp cooler blowing hot air?

Most common causes: a failed pump (pads aren't getting wet), clogged distribution tubing from hard water, outside temps above ~105°F, low fan speed, or worn pads that need replacement.

What does evaporative cooler repair cost in Gilbert or Mesa?

Typical ranges: pad replacement $75–$150, pump $100–$200, belt $75–$125, motor $200–$450, spring startup $75–$175. See our full cost guide.
