Gilbert has grown from a small farming town of 5,700 people in 1980 to one of the fastest-growing cities in the United States, now home to over 270,000 residents. High calcium and magnesium levels accelerate pad mineralization and pump wear faster than in many surrounding cities. If your pads seem to need replacement every season, that's why. Common Swamp Cooler Issues in Gilbert

Gilbert's notoriously hard water is the dominant maintenance factor for swamp coolers in this area. Calcium and magnesium deposits clog distribution tubes faster than in softer-water communities, reducing water flow to pads and causing dry-pad hot-air issues even when the pump appears to be running. Local providers familiar with Gilbert commonly check distribution lines, pump impellers, and pads for mineral buildup.

  • Unit running but blowing warm or room-temperature air
  • Cooler not turning on at all
  • Grinding, squealing, or rattling sounds
  • Water leaking or pooling around the unit
  • Pads visibly brown, crusted, or moldy
  • Reduced airflow even on high speed
  • Motor running but fan not spinning
