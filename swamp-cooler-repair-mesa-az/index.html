Common Swamp Cooler Issues in Mesa

Mesa's tap water is moderately to highly mineralized, depending on your water district and neighborhood. This means cooling pad lifespan is shorter than in lower-mineral areas — many Mesa homeowners replace aspen pads every season rather than every two years. The I-60 corridor also sees more dust and particulate buildup in filters and pads than newer suburban areas further east.

  • Unit running but blowing warm or room-temperature air
  • Cooler not turning on at all
  • Grinding, squealing, or rattling sounds
  • Water leaking or pooling around the unit
  • Pads visibly brown, crusted, or moldy
  • Reduced airflow even on high speed
  • Motor running but fan not spinning
